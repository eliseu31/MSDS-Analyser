=====================  Hazards Identification  =====================

Routes of Entry: Inhalation:YES  Skin:YES  Ingestion:NO
Reports of Carcinogenicity:NTP:NO    IARC:NO	OSHA:NO
Health Hazards Acute and Chronic:ACUTE: VAPORS IRRIT TO EYES, NOSE,
    THROAT. INHALATION MAY CAUSE HEADACHE, DIFFICULT BREATHING, LOSS OF
    CONSCIOUSNESS. CHRONIC: AS A RESULT OF PREVIOUS REPEATED OVEREXPOS
    OR A SINGLE LARGE DOSE, MAY DE VELOP ISOCYANATE SENSITIZATION-LATER
    REACTION TO EXPOSURE OF ISOCYANATE LEVELS WELL BELOW TLV VALUE OF
    ISOCYANATES.
Effects of Overexposure:INHALE: RESP TRACT IRRIT & ACUTE NERV SYST
    DEPRESS-HEADACHE, DIZZINESS, STAGGERING GAIT, CONFUSION,
    UNCONSCIOUSNESS, COMA. SKIN: IRRIT-SWELLING, REDNESS, RASH,
    CRACKING. EYES: IRRIT-TEARING, REDNESS,  SWELLING, STINGING
    SENSATION. INGEST: IRRIT, POSS CORROSIVE TO MOUTH, STOMACH TISSUE,
    DIGESTIVE TRACT. CHEM PNEUMONITIS.
Medical Cond Aggravated by Exposure:ASTHMA & ANY OTHER RESPIRATORY
    DISORDERS, SKIN ALLERGIES, ECZEMA, DERMATITIS. ISOCYANATE
    SENSITIZATION.

=======================  First Aid Measures  =======================

First Aid:INHALE: MOVE TO FRESH AIR. RESTORE BREATHING. ASTHMATIC-TYPE
    SYMPTOMS MAY DEVELOP IMMED OR BE DELAYED. GET MED AID. SKIN: REMOVE
    CONTAM CLOTHING & WASH BEFORE REUSE. WASH AFFECTED AREAS THOROUGHLY
    W/S OAP & WATER. EYES: FLUSH W/CLEAN, LUKEWARM WATER (LOW PRESS)-15
    MIN., OCCAS LIFTING EYELIDS. GET MED AID. INGESTION: DO NOT INDUCE
    VOMIT OR GIVE ANYTHING BY MOUTH TO UNCONSCIOUS PERSON. GET MED AID.

=====================  Fire Fighting Measures  =====================

Flash Point Method:TCC
Flash Point:24.0F,-4.4C
Lower Limits:1.
Extinguishing Media:USE WATER FOG, CARBON DIOXIDE, FOAM, OR DRY
    CHEMICAL.
Fire Fighting Procedures:FULL FIRE FIGHT EQUIP W/SCBA & FULL PROTECT
    CLOTH. WTR--COOL CLOSED CNTNRS/PREVENT: PRESS BLD-UP/AUTO
    IGNITE/EXPLOSION. DURING FIRE, HDI/IRRIT VAPORS GENERATED.
Unusual Fire/Explosion Hazard:CNTNRE CLSED-MAY EXPLODE/HIGH HEAT. NO
    HEAT/SPARKS/ELECTRIC EQUIP/OPEN FLAME. APPLI TO HOT SURFACES
    SPECIAL PRECAUTIONS. EXPOSURE TO DECOMP PRODS HEALTH HAZARD.

==================  Accidental Release Measures  ==================

Spill Release Procedures:EVACUATE ALL NONESSENTIAL PERSONNEL. REMOVE
    ALL SOURCES OF IGNITION (FLAME, SPARK SOURCES, HOT SURFACES).
    VENTILATE AREA. CONTAIN AND REMOVE WITH INERT ABSORBENT &
    NON-SPARKING TOOLS.

======================	Handling and Storage  ======================

Handling and Storage Precautions:STORE IN BUILDINGS DESIGNED TO COMPLY
    W/OSHA 1910.106. AVOID STORING NEAR IGNITE SOURCES. STORE IN
    TIGHTLY CLOSED CONTAINRS IN WELL-VENTILATED AREAS.
Other Precautions:KEEP UPRIGHT TO PREVENT LEAKAGE. PREVENT PROLONGED OR
    REPEATED BREATHING OF VAPORS OR SPRAY MISTS. PROLONGED OVEREXPOSURE
    MAY CAUSE AN ALLERGIC REACTION. AVOID CONTACT W/SKIN & EYES. DO NOT
    TAKE INTER NALLY.

=============  Exposure Controls/Personal Protection  =============

Respiratory Protection:RSPRTR RECOMM/APPROVE--USE IN ORGANIC VAPOR
    ENVIRON (AIR PURIFYING/FRESH AIR). OBSERVE OSHA REGS--RESPIRATOR
    USE. VENT TO KEEP EXPOSURE LEVELS < OSHA LIMITS. VAPOR PARTIC
    RSPRTR (TC23C--NIOSH) IF VAPO R LEVELS BELOW 10 X EXPOSURE LEVELS.
Ventilation:EXHAUST VENT SUFF TO KEEP AIRBORNE CONC OF SOLVENT, HDI,
    POLYISOCYANATES BELOW RESPECTIVE TLV'S. REMOVE IGNITE SOURCES.
Protective Gloves:NEOPRENE, RUBBER, POLYETHYLENE, COTTON.
Eye Protection:CHEM GOGGLES,SIDE/FACE SHLD,SPLASH GUARD
Other Protective Equipment:USE LONG SLEEVE AND LONG LEG CLOTHING.
    REMOVE AND WASH CONTAMINATED CLOTHING BEFORE REUSE.
Work Hygienic Practices:WASH HANDS BEFORE EATING, SMOKING OR USING
    WASHROOM. SMOKE IN SMOKING AREAS ONLY.
Supplemental Safety and Health
DO NOT HANDLE UNTIL THE MANUFACTURER'S SAFETY PRECAUTIONS HAVE BEEN
    READ AND UNDERSTOOD. APPLICATION TO HOT SURFACES REQUIRES SPECIAL
    PRECAUTIONS.
