=====================  Hazards Identification  =====================

LD50 LC50 Mixture:NONE SPECIFIED BY MANUFACTURER.
Routes of Entry: Inhalation:YES  Skin:YES  Ingestion:YES
Reports of Carcinogenicity:NTP:NO    IARC:YES	 OSHA:NO
Health Hazards Acute and Chronic:KOH CONTACT WITH SKIN OR EYES MAY
    CAUSE SEVERE IRRITATION OR BURNS. INGESTION OF KOH MAY RESULT IN
    SEVER INTESTINAL IRRITATION WITH BURNS TO MOUTH. CADMIUM POISONING
    CAUSES LUNG AND KIDNEY INJURY. CAD MIUM IS A SUSPECT CARCINOGEN.
    NIOSH RECOMMENDS THAT NICKEL BE TREATED AS AN OCCUPATIONAL
    CARCINOGEN.
Explanation of Carcinogenicity:CADMIUM OXIDE: IARC GRP 2A, NTP GRP 2:
    NICKEL HYDROXIDE: IARC GRP 1, NTP GRP 2; NICKEL: IARC GRP 1, NTP
    GRP 2.
Effects of Overexposure:SEE HEALTH HAZARDS.
Medical Cond Aggravated by Exposure:INGEST OF KOH MAY RESULT IN SEVERE
    INTESTINAL IRRITATION WITH BURNS TO MOUTH.

=======================  First Aid Measures  =======================

First Aid:EYES: FLUSH WITH COPIOUS AMOUNTS OF WATER FOR AT LEAST 15
    MINUTES. GET MD. SKIN: FLUSH WITH COPIOUS AMOUNTS OF WATER FOR AT
    LEAST 15 MINUTES. GET MD. INHAL:REMOVE TO FRESH AIR. SUPPORT
    BREATHING (GIVE  O2/ARTF RESP). INGEST: DO NOT INDUCE VOMITING.
    GIVE LARGE AMOUNTS OF WATER FOLLOWED BY DILUTED VINEGAR, FRUIT
    JUICE, OR WHITES OF EGGS.

=====================  Fire Fighting Measures  =====================

Extinguishing Media:DRY CHEMICAL, FOAM.
Fire Fighting Procedures:USE NIOSH/MSHA APPROVED SCBA AND FULL
    PROTECTIVE CLOTHING IF INVOLVED IN FIRE.
Unusual Fire/Explosion Hazard:POLYPHENYLENE OXIDE EVOLVES CARBON
    MONOXIDE WHEN BURNED.

==================  Accidental Release Measures  ==================

Spill Release Procedures:WEAR NIOSH/MSHA APPROVED SCBA AND FULL PROT
    CLOTHING. VENTILATE AREA. NEUTRALIZE SPILL SLOWLY WITH DILUTE
    HYDROCHOLORIC, ACETIC OR BORIC ACID. FLUSH AREA WITH FLOODING
    AMOUNTS OF WATER.
Neutralizing Agent:DILUTE HYDROCHLORIC, ACETIC OR BORIC ACID.
