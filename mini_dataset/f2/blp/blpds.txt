=====================  Hazards Identification  =====================

Routes of Entry: Inhalation:YES  Skin:YES  Ingestion:NO
Reports of Carcinogenicity:NTP:NO    IARC:NO	OSHA:NO
Health Hazards Acute and Chronic:EXPOSURE TO GLASS FIBERS MAY PRODUCE
    ITCHING, RASH, CONJUNCTIVITIS, COUGHING OR SNEEZING.  THEY ARE
    PRIMARILY AN INHALLATION HAZARD.
Explanation of Carcinogenicity:PRODUCT IS CONTINUOUS FILAMENT GLASS
    WHICH IS LISTED BY IARC AS CLASS 3 'INSUFFICIENT EVIDENCE TO
    CLASSIFY". SEE SUPP DA
Effects of Overexposure:ITCHING OR RASH OF SKIN WHERE CONTACTED. 
    COUGHING OR SNEEZING IF FIBERS ARE INHALED.
Medical Cond Aggravated by Exposure:NONE GIVEN BY MANUFACTURER
    (SUPPLIER).

=======================  First Aid Measures  =======================

First Aid:EYE:FLUSH W/WATER 15 MIN, HOLD LIDS OPEN. SKIN:WASH WITH SOAP
    & WATER. INHALED:REMOVE FROM EXPOSURE.   IF FIBER GLASS BECOMES
    IMBEDED IN THE SKIN, SEE A DOCTOR.
