Health Hazards Acute and Chronic:SWALLOWING: GASTROINTESTINAL
    IRRITATION. INHALATION: NOSE OR THROAT IRRITATION. EYE: MAY CAUSE
    EYE IRRITATION. SKIN: MAY CAUSE DEFATTING AND IRRITATION OF THE
    SKIN. PRIMARY SKIN IRRITANT.
Explanation of Carcinogenicity:DI(2-ETHYLHEXYL) PHTHALATE IS LISTED AS
    A POTENTIAL CARCINOGEN BY THE NTP.
Effects of Overexposure:OVEREXPOSURE TO TOLUENE MAY CAUSE LIVER DAMAGE.
    OVEREXPOSURE TO SOLVENT WITH PERMANENT BRAIN AND NERVOUS SYSTEM
    DAMAGE. INTENTIONAL MISUSE BY CONCENTRATION AND INHALING THE
    CONTENTS MAY BE HARMFUL OR	FATAL.

=======================  First Aid Measures  =======================

First Aid:SWALLOWINTG: DO NOT INDUCE VOMITING. INHALATION REMOVE TO
    FRESH AIR IMMEDIATELY.EYE: FLUSH WITH LARGE AMOUNTS OF WATER
    LIFTING UPPER AND LOWER LIDS. SKIN: IMMEDIATELY FLUSH THE
    CONTAMINATED AREA WITH  LARGE AMOUNTS OF WATER. REMOVE CONTAMINATED
    CLOTHING AS WATER IS APPLIED. GET MED ATTN ON ALL OF THE ABOVE.
    NOTE: TREATMENT FOR ABOVE S/B DIRECTED AT THE CONTROL OF SYMPTOMS
    AND THE CLINICAL CONDITION

=====================  Fire Fighting Measures  =====================

Flash Point:20F -6.67C
Extinguishing Media:NFPA CLASS B FIRE EXTINGUISHER DESIGNED TO
    EXTINGUISH FLAMMABLE LIQUID FIRES. POLYMER FOAM PREFERRED FOR LARGE
    FIRES.

    MAY BE INNEFECTIVE, BUT MAY BE USED TO PREVENT PRESSURE BUILD-UP.

    MAY CAUSE HEALTH HAZARD. OBTAIN MEDICAL ATTENTION. WARNING:

==================  Accidental Release Measures  ==================
