=====================  Hazards Identification  =====================

Routes of Entry: Inhalation:YES  Skin:YES  Ingestion:YES
Reports of Carcinogenicity:NTP:NO    IARC:NO	OSHA:YES
Health Hazards Acute and Chronic:EYE: MAY CAUSE REDNESS AND IRRITATION
    TO THE EYES; SKIN: MAY CAUSE REDNESS & IRRITATION TO THE SKIN;
    INHALE:MAY CAUSE BRONCHIOLITIS,PNEUMONITIS PULMONARY EDENA IF LARGE
    AMOUNTS IS INHALED; INGEST:MAY  CAUSE NEUROTOXIC/NEPHROTOXIC
    EFFECTS.
Explanation of Carcinogenicity:MERCURY IS OSHA REGULATED.
Effects of Overexposure:CHRONIC MERCURY POISONING RESULTS IN NERVOUS
    IRRITABILITY, WEAKNESS, TREMORS, GINGIVITIS, ERETHISM AND GREYING
    OF LENS OF THE EYE.
Medical Cond Aggravated by Exposure:NONE SPECIFIED BY MANUFACTURER.

=======================  First Aid Measures  =======================

First Aid:EYE:WASH THOROUGHLY WITH WATER FOR 15 MINUTES HOLDING UPPER
    AND LOWER EYELIDS. CONSULT A PHYSICIAN. SKIN: WASH THOROUGLY WITH
    SOAP & WATER. CONSULT A PHYSICIAN. INHALE: REMOVE TO FEESH AIR.
    CONSULT A  PHYSICIAN. INGEST: CONSULT A PHYSICIAN IMMEDIATELY.

=====================  Fire Fighting Measures  =====================

Unusual Fire/Explosion Hazard:HIGH TEMPERATURES INCRESASE THE
    VAPORIZATION OF MERCURY.

==================  Accidental Release Measures  ==================

Spill Release Procedures:SWEEP UP AS MUCH SPILLAGE AS POSSIBLE USING
    ABSORBENT MATERIAL. VACUUM AREA USING SPECIAL MERCURY VACUUM
    CLEANER.
Neutralizing Agent:NONE

======================	Handling and Storage  ======================

Handling and Storage Precautions:EATING OR SMOKING SHOULD NOT BE
    PERMITTED IN AREAS WHERE MERCURY IS HANDLED OR STORED.
Other Precautions:USE ACCORDING TO DIRECTIONS.

=============  Exposure Controls/Personal Protection  =============

Respiratory Protection:WHEN WORKING IN AREAS WHICH EXCEED TLV, WEAR
    DUST MASK WITH APPROVED CARTREIDGE FOR MERCURY VAPOR.
Ventilation:LOCAL EXHAUST IS RECOMMENDED. USE IN A WELL VENTILATED
    AREA.
Protective Gloves:MAY USE IMPERVIOUS GLOVES.
Eye Protection:MAY USE SAFETY GLASSES.
Other Protective Equipment:MAY USE LAB COAT OR APRON.
Work Hygienic Practices:USE GOOD CHEMICAL HYGIENE PRACTICE. AVOID
    UNNECESSARY CONTACT. WASH THOROUGHLY BEFORE EATING OR DRINKING.
Supplemental Safety and Health
EMPLOYEES WHO HANDLE MERCURY SHOULD WASH THEIR HANDS THOROUHLY WITH
    SOAP AND WATER BEFORE EATING, SMOKING OR USING THE TOILET. THIS
    MSDS REFLECTS THE HAZARDS OF THE MERCURY PART OF THE PRODUCT AS THE
     POWDER IS CONSIDERED NON-HAZARDOUS ABY THE MANUFACTURER.
