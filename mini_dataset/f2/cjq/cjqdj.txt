=====================  Hazards Identification  =====================

Reports of Carcinogenicity:NTP:NO    IARC:NO	OSHA:NO
Health Hazards Acute and Chronic:POTENTIAL HEALTH EFFECTS: ACUTE: EYE
    CONT: CAUSES EYE IRRITATION. SKIN CONT: CAUSES SKIN IRRITATION.
    INGEST: MAY CAUSE IRRITATION TO MOUTH, ESOPHAGUS, AND STOMACH.
    INHAL: INHALATION OF SPRAY MIST CAUS ES IRRITATION TO RESPIRATORY
    TRACT. SIGNS & SYMPTOMS OF EXPOSURE: EYES: PAIN, REDNESS, AND
    TEARING. SKIN: REDNESS, ITCHING OR BURNING. INGEST: BITTER TASTE,
    PAIN IN MOUTH OR DIGESTIVE TRACT, NAUSEA. I NHAL: SNEEZING,
    COUGHING, BURNING OR ITCHING IN NOSE AND THROAT. POTENTIAL PHYSICAL
    HAZARDS: CHRONIC: REPEATED INGESTION OF LARGE QUANTITIES (>1 GRAM)
    MAY CAUSE KIDNEY STONES OR OTHER KIDNEY CHANGES.  (EFTS OF OVEREXP)
Explanation of Carcinogenicity:THERE ARE NO KNOWN REPORTS OF
    CARCINOGENICITY OF SODIUM SILICATES.
Effects of Overexposure:HLTH HAZS: DRIES TO FORM GLASS FILM WHICH CAN
    EASILY CUT SKIN. SPILLS ARE VERY SLIPPERY. CAN ETCH GLASS IF NOT
    PROMPTLY REMOVED. TARGET ORGANS: ACUTE ORAL LETHALITY RSLTS FROM
    NONSPECIFIC CAUSES. TARG ET ORGANS FOR ACUTE SKIN, EYE, &
    INHALATION EXPOSURE ARE THE SITES OF EXPOSURE; NO SYSTEMIC TOXICITY
    IS OBSERVED.
Medical Cond Aggravated by Exposure:INHALATION OF SPRAY MIST MAY
    AGGRAVATE ASTHMA AND LUNG DISEASES. CONTACT WITH SKIN MAY AGGRAVATE
    PREEXISTING SKIN CONDITIONS.

=======================  First Aid Measures  =======================

First Aid:INHAL: REMOVE TO FRESH AIR. IF NOT BRTHG, GIVE ARTF RESP. IF
    BRTHG IS DFCLT, GIVE OXYGEN. EYE CONT: IMMEDIATELY FLUSH W/PLENTY
    OF WATER FOR AT LST 15 MINUTES. CALL PHYS. SKIN CONT: IMMEDIATELY
    FLUSH W /PLENTY OF WATER. REMOVE CONTAMD CLOTHING & SHOES. GET
    MEDICAL ATTN. WASH CLOTHING BEFORE REUSE. THOROUGHLY CLEAN SHOES
    BEFORE REUSE. INGEST: DO NOT INDUE VOMITING. GET MEDICAL ATTN
    IMMEDIATELY. IF VI CTIM IS FULLY CONSCIOUS, GIVE CUPFUL OF WATER.
    NEVER GIVE ANYTHING BY MOUTH TO UNCONSCIOUS PERSON.

    32-140. AVOID CONT W/EYES, SKIN & CLTHG. AVOID BRTHG SPRAY MIST.
    KEEP CNTNR CLSD. PROMPTLY CLEAN RESIDUE FROM CLOSURES W/CLOTH

    FROM ACIDS, REACTIVE METALS, AND AMMONIUM SALTS. SPEC SENSITIVITY:
    DO NOT STORE IN ALUMINUM, FIBERGLASS, COPPER, BRASS, ZINC OR
    GALVANIZED CONTAIN ERS.

===================  Toxicological Information	===================

Toxicological Information:LIFETIME FEEDING STUDY OF ZEOLITE A (WHICH
    RAPIDLY DECOMPOSES TO AMORPHOUS SODIUM ALUMINATES & SODIUM SILICATE
    IN STOM) CONCLUDED THAT CHRONIC FEEDING @ DOSES OF 0.001, 0.01, &
    0.1% IN DIET OF RATS DI D NOT PRDCE CANCER/CHRONIC ORGAN TOX.
    FREQUENT INGEST OVER EXTENDED PERIODS OF TIME OF GRAM QTYS OF
    SILICATES IS ASSOC W/FORMATION KIDNEY STONE & OTHER SILICEOUS
    URINARY CALCULI IN HUMANS. MUTAGENCITY : SODIUM SILICATE WAS NOT
    MUTAGENIC TO BACTERIUM E. COLI WHEN TESTED IN MUTAGENICITY
    BIOASSAY. REPRODUCTION: ONE STUDY RPTD DECR NUMBERS OF BIRTHS
    (TRANSPORT INFO)

=====================  Ecological Information  =====================

Ecological:CHEM FATE INFO: DILUTE MATL RAPIDLY DEPOLYMERIZES TO YIELD
    DISSOLVED SILICA IN FORM THAT IS INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM NATRL
    DISSOLVED SILICA. DOES NOT CONTRIBUTE TO BOD. DISTRIBUTION: ONLY
    WATER CONTENT  WILL EVAP TO AIR. WHERE ABNORMALLY LOW NATRL SILICA
    CONCS EXIST (<0.1PPM), DISSOLVED SILICA MAY BE LIMITIING NUTRIENT
    FOR DIATOMS & FEW OTHER AQUATIC ALGAL SPECIES. HOWEVER, ADDN OF
    EXCESS DISSOLVED S ILICA OVER LIMITING CONC WILL NOT STIMULATE
    GROWTH OF DIATOM POPULATIONS; THEIR GROWTH RATE IS INDEPENDENT OF
    SILICA CONC ONCE LIMITING CONC IS EXCEEDED. (SARA TITLE III)
